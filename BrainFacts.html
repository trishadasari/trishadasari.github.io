Fact #1:

The average adult human brain weighs 3 pounds and has a texture like firm jelly.

Fact #2:

The size of the brain doesn’t matter. No evidence exists that says that a larger brain is smarter than a smaller brain.

Fact #3:

There are 100,000 miles of blood vessels in the brain. The distance around the world at the equator is 24,900 miles.

Fact #4:

Every time your heart beats, your arteries carry 20 to 25 percent of your blood to the brain.

Fact #5:

Every time you recall a memory or have a new thought, you create a connection in the brain.

Fact #6:

The harder you think, the more oxygen and fuel your brain will use from your blood – up to 50 percent.

Fact #7:

The human brain is about 75% water.

Fact #8:

More electrical impulses are generated in one day by a single human brain than by all the telephones in the world.

Fact #9:

Your brain consumes about 25 watts of power while you’re awake – enough energy to illuminate a lightbulb!

Fact #10:

70,000 is the number of thoughts estimated that the human brain produces on an average day.

Fact #11:

The brain can’t feel any sort of pain.

Fact #12:

Cholesterol is key to learning and memory. However, high cholesterol has different effects depending on your age and other factors.

Fact #13:

Headaches are caused by a chemical reaction in your brain combined with the muscles and nerves of your neck and head.

Fact #14:

The human brain will grow three times its size in the first year of life. It continues to grow until you’re about 18 years old.

Fact #15:

The human brain gets smaller as we get older. This usually happens sometime after middle age.
